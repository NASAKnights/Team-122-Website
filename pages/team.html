Meet the Team!

Mechanical

The mechanical team is a combination of CAD design and fabrication, working with industry standard software to design and assess the robot structure. The team as a whole will brainstorm and come up with ideas for the season, but the mechanical team will be the ones to flesh out the fesability of the ideas as well as prototyping simple mechanisms.

Students learn to use SolidWorks, Inventor etc, in order to create computer renderings of the robot to check fesability as well being able to visualize the design before committing to using better materials for the final build.

Electrical

The electrical team is responsible for the control systems, as well as the wiring of all the mechanical components installed. The control system is how the robot is able to interact with the rest of the world. Sensors provide the robot with different types of information, such as when a mechanism has reached the limit of its motion, or vision information about it's environment.

They also are responsible for connecting all the components together and making sure that such connections do not hinder the mechanical movements. Electrical solves the puzzle of how to build and work around pre-built structures while adding new capabilities. They also make sure the robot is in compliance with FRC rules and regulations, such as pressure limits and power draw. They work closely with the rest of the team to ensure all parties get what they want and need.

Control Systems

The control systems team are the ones who really bridge the gap between the hardware and software. They help with the calculations that explain how the robot moves in the real world. Control System students need to have a strong understanding of math and physics to approach simulating the robot. They help to decide what type of gearing is needed, whether we need more torque versus more speed.

They are responsible for figuring out necessary sensors and how those sensors work together to get the best profile for every mechanism.

Programming

Programming is responsible for developing and updating the robot's software, as well as processing the information provided by the control system. The programming team uses different languages in order to bring the robot to life, with a current focus on Java, C++ and python. The team works with different computer topics such as computer vision, open and closed loop control, human-computer interaction and many others.

The programming team is what makes all the different components move, such as the motors and different actuators. They work with the drive team in order to create a better interface for ease of access, and to make controlling the robot as simple as possible.

Programming also is responsible for other pieces of software, such as this website!

Business

Team branding! Awards! Sponsorships! Outreach! This multi-talented team is responsible for promoting the team, both in and out of community. This team knows the ins and outs of how the team works and makes sure that we're well-supplied to do so. They help to take videos and photos during different events, collecting pictures of both the team and robot for documentation.

The business team is comprised of various subteams which help with different aspects of business ranging from documentation, award/sponsorship writing, social media wrangling etc.

Drive Team

This is a group of 3-4 students and a coach, who may also be a student. This group is the main interface of the team during competitions, responsible for communicating with the other teams on the alliance, as well as responsible for controlling the robot during gameplay.

They work closely with the pit crew, reporting back any abnormal behaviors, as well as tracking performance of the robot over matches. They are to have a strong general understanding of the robot and all its components, as well as a firm understanding of the game. They must be able to follow the given rules as well as be able to adapt to different strategies as necessary.

Pit

Robot breaks down during competitions, and the pit team are the ones to the rescue! The pit team is responsible for knowing the ins and outs of the robot, from the big idea to the little tiny fasteners that hold each part together. They work closely with the drive team to document needed repairs and possible upgrades and changes that arise during competition. The students are also responsible for making sure anyone, mentor or student, is working safely around the robot.

Whether the robot is on the field or off, the pit crew is also responsible for talking with the judges and other teams that come around. They not only know about the robot, but are able to communicate that knowledge clearly.

Scouting

Scouting is an integral part of the team, responsible for gathering data during competitions. They help to develop the strategy for the team in the heat of the competitions, having observed every robot in every match. Scouts must have a clear understanding of the game in order to analyze the data gathered, as well as have a strong understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the team's robot to form the best alliance possible.
