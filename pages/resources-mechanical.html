4. Subsystems

Robots are made up of multiple subsystems that work together to meet the specific challenges of each year's game. Below are some of the most common subsystems, along with resources to help you learn about their design and implementation.

4.1 Articulated Arm

An articulated arm is a rotating mechanism used to position game pieces or other subsystems for scoring. This subsystem offers versatility, making it useful for a variety of tasks.
2024 Unqualified Quokkas Ri3D
2024 Team 2910 - Telescoping Arm with wrist
Read RAP Robotics Design Guide for detailed information

4.2 Elevator

Elevators are linear mechanisms designed to move a carriage (often carrying another subsystem). They are commonly used to lift objects to varying heights for scoring.
2024 Team 148
Read RAP Robotics Design Guide for detailed information

4.3 Intake

The intake is one of the most crucial subsystems, responsible for quickly and reliably collecting game pieces. Effective designs ensure fast and controlled intake performance.
2024 Cranberry Alarm Ri3D
2024 Team 118
2022 Team 254
Read RAP Robotics Design Guide for detailed information

4.4 Shooter

Shooters are used to launch game pieces into a scoring area. Common types include flywheel shooters, catapults, and linear punches, each offering unique advantages depending on the challenge.
2022 Team 254
Read RAP Robotics Design Guide for detailed information

4.5 Turret

A turret allows another subsystem, often a shooter, to rotate and aim at targets from different positions on the field. While turrets provide increased flexibility, they require careful design and integration.

4.6 Climber

Climbers are typically arms or other mechanisms used to lift the robot onto a structure, usually as part of the endgame. Effective climbers need to be strong, stable, and reliable under stress. Read RAP Robotics Design Guide for detailed information
